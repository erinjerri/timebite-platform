## Algorithms Used

- **Transformer Encoder-Decoder**:  
  Whisper utilizes the “sequence-to-sequence” (seq2seq) transformer architecture. Both input (audio) and output (text) sequences are processed through stacked layers of multi-head self-attention and feed-forward neural networks.
- **Audio Feature Extraction**:  
  Audio is converted into log-Mel spectrograms as a pre-processing step before being fed into the model.
- **Teacher Forcing**:  
  Used during training for faster, more stable convergence in autoregressive decoding.
- **Byte Pair Encoding (BPE)**:  
  Whisper tokenizes transcriptions using a BPE subword vocabulary.
- **Beam Search Decoding**:  
  For inference, Whisper uses beam search to generate transcription hypotheses, balancing between breadth and depth in exploring output sequences.
- **Language ID Conditioning**:  
  Whisper optionally uses special tokens to prompt language identification or other tasks.

## Architecture Pipeline

```
Audio Input
    ↓
Preprocessing
    - Downsample to 16kHz
    - Convert to mono
    - Compute 80-channel log-Mel spectrogram (typically 30s window)
    ↓
Encoder (Transformer)
    - Processes spectrogram frames
    - Stacked multi-head self-attention + FFNN
    - Encodes audio context into hidden representations
    ↓
Decoder (Transformer)
    - Autoregressively generates text tokens
    - Attends to both previous generated tokens (causal self-attention)
    - Attends to encoder outputs (cross-attention)
    ↓
Text Output (Token Sequence)
    - Detokenized into readable string
```

**Details:**
- Both encoder and decoder use positional encodings.
- Decoder input is shifted right with a start-of-transcript token; output is generated token by token.
- Special tokens can trigger translation, language detection, timestamps, etc.

## Performance Considerations

- **Model Size & Inference Speed**:  
  Whisper ranges from “Tiny” (~39M params) to “Large” (~1.55B params). Larger models = better accuracy but slower inference and heavier memory/compute requirements.
- **Real-Time Capabilities**:  
  Large models may require batching, quantization, or distillation for deployment in latency-sensitive applications.
- **Audio Segmentation**:  
  Whisper processes ~30 seconds at a time—longer inputs must be chunked and stitched together.
- **Resource Use**:  
  Strong GPU acceleration (NVIDIA CUDA) is highly recommended for real-time use, especially for large models.
- **Multilingual/Multitask**:  
  Whisper supports many languages and tasks, but performance may vary depending on language/resource balance.
- **Robustness**:  
  Whisper is robust to varied audio, including noisy, accented, or non-standard speech, due to its diverse training data. Still, edge cases (rare languages, domain-specific jargon) can be challenging.

## Implementation Suggestions

- **Hardware**:  
  Use NVIDIA GPUs with at least 8GB VRAM for real-time large model inference; CPUs are feasible for small models or offline batch processing.
- **Streaming**:  
  For live applications, segment input and overlap slightly to account for context at chunk boundaries. Output can be emitted per-chunk with post-assembly.
- **Batching**:  
  Aggregate multiple audio requests per inference batch for throughput optimization.
- **Quantization/Pruning**:  
  Use INT8 or mixed-precision models for deployment to reduce model size and latency, with minor accuracy trade-offs.
- **Serving & APIs**:  
  Use FastAPI, Flask, or the Hugging Face Inference Endpoints for exposing models as production-ready services.
- **Custom Tokenizer**:  
  If your domain uses unique vocabulary (e.g. medical or legal), consider additional fine-tuning with a domain-adapted tokenizer and data.
- **Monitoring**:  
  Continuously log and review failed or low-confidence transcriptions for targeted improvement.
