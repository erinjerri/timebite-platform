# Brand Positioning (starter)

TimeBite’s brand should feel calm, high-agency, and grounded in execution truth.

## One-sentence positioning
TimeBite is a local-first focus operating system that turns daily intent into execution truth with ring-based progress you can feel.

## Who it’s for
- Founders, PMs, and builders who value deep work and intentional time allocation.

## The pain we solve
- Reactive days
- Context switching
- “Planned” work that never becomes real execution
- Progress that’s invisible until the end of the week

## Key differentiators
- **Time budgets, not just tasks:** rings represent minutes and attention.
- **Execution truth:** session logs drive analytics, not wishful checkmarks.
- **Local-first privacy:** your reflections and notes stay yours by default.
- **Small surface area:** 3–4 lanes, one “now” timer.

## Message pillars (starter)
1. **Intentional time**
2. **Focused execution**
3. **Truthful reflection**
4. **Private by default**

## Tone and voice
- Calm, direct, non-preachy.
- High agency: “Choose”, “Commit”, “Start”, “Finish”, “Learn”.
- Avoid productivity guilt framing.
- Use words like "Table (for later), and have optionality of "returning to projects" as a reminder notification for user that has the ability to archive old projects, merge them, and create new ones.
- The overall tone should be ADHD and neurodivergent friendly, not forced into linear thinking, but should be able to be flexible enough and provide for the user to have some breathing room in their own productivity stack without becoming overly disorganized.
  
## Sample taglines (starter)
- “Make time feel real.”
- “Intent → execution.”
- “A calm OS for your day.”
- “Rings that protect focus.”

## Visual direction (starter)
- Warm neutrals + lane color tokens.
- Soft depth, rectangular cards, minimal chrome.
- Rings/torus as the hero motif, but avoid sci‑fi overload for iPhone for performance issues.
